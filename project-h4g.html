Hack4Good 2025

The Problem

The Muhammadiyah Welfare Home needed a modernized, digital system to handle the distribution of products to its residents. Tracking inventory, managing resident allowances, and handling transactions manually is inefficient and prone to errors.

The Solution

During the Hack4Good 2025 hackathon, I helped develop a web-based minimart application. This platform allows residents to securely purchase products using a digital voucher system, completely streamlining the home's internal economy.

Technical Implementation

  • Frontend Development: Built responsive and intuitive resident and administrator interfaces using Vue.js.
  • Backend & Database: Integrated Firebase for real-time database management and secure user authentication.
  • Digital Vouchers: Designed a secure logic flow for residents to redeem products against their allocated digital voucher balances.
